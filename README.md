New Jersey (Northeast Region) Sales Territory Analysis

Project Overview:

This project is part of my capstone work, where I conducted a detailed analysis of in-store sales performance within the New Jersey territory, which falls under the Northeast region.

The main objective was to use SQL to extract, analyse, and interpret sales data in order to identify trends, evaluate store performance, and develop practical, data-driven business recommendations.

To maintain clarity and focus, I limited my analysis strictly to store sales, excluding online transactions. This allowed me to directly assess how physical store locations are performing across the New Jersey market.

Objectives
Calculate the total revenue generated from New Jersey store sales
Analyse revenue trends over time (monthly breakdown)
Compare performance across individual store locations
Evaluate transaction volume and average transaction size
Identify top-performing and underperforming stores
Assess product category performance
Develop a data-driven recommendation for the next quarter
Key Questions
What is the total revenue for New Jersey store sales, and what time period does the data cover?
What trends can be observed in monthly revenue performance?
How does performance vary across different store locations?
What are the transaction counts and average transaction values by product category?
Which stores are performing at the highest and lowest levels?
Based on the analysis, where should the business focus next quarter?
Data & Tools Used

Database: MySQL

Tables Used:

-store_sales
-store_locations
-products
-inventory_categories

SQL Techniques Applied:

-JOIN operations to connect related datasets
-Filtering using WHERE clauses (focused on New Jersey)
-Aggregations using SUM, COUNT, and AVG
-GROUP BY for structured analysis
-ORDER BY for ranking and trend evaluation
-Key Insights

Through my analysis, I identified a significant performance gap between stores within the New Jersey territory. Some locations consistently generate strong revenue, while others lag.

I also observed that product category performance is uneven. Certain categories contribute a large portion of total revenue, while others show relatively low sales activity. This indicates that not all product lines are equally effective in driving revenue.

Overall, the data highlights clear opportunities to improve performance by focusing on weaker areas while leveraging existing strengths.

Recommendation

Based on my findings, I recommend that the sales manager prioritise improving underperforming stores in the upcoming quarter. 

These locations may benefit from:

-More targeted promotions
-Better inventory management
-Improved product placement
-Increased staffing or customer engagement strategies

Additionally, low-performing product categories should be reviewed to determine whether the issue is due to demand, visibility, or stock availability.

At the same time, top-performing stores should be analysed as benchmarks. Understanding what drives their success can help replicate those strategies across weaker locations.

Next Quarter Focus:

-Improve performance of low-revenue stores
-Strengthen underperforming product categories
-Apply best practices from top-performing stores
-Increase both total revenue and average transaction size

Conclusion:

This project demonstrates how SQL can be used to transform raw data into meaningful business insights. By identifying performance gaps and applying targeted strategies, the business can improve overall sales across the New Jersey territory.

This analysis does not only highlights current performance but also provides insights for data-driven decision-making moving forward.
